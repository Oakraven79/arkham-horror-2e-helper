# Expansion Rules

Expansion behavior is opt-in. Do not let a component, threshold, deck, or
trigger leak into a game where its parent expansion or named variant is
disabled.

## Shared expansion-board rules

The expansion boards are Dunwich, Kingsport, and Innsmouth.

### Modified count for multiple boards

With more than one expansion board:

`board count = max(1, actual investigator count - (expansion boards - 1))`

The modified count cannot fall below 1. It is used only for:

- the Arkham monster limit;
- the Outskirts maximum;
- the open-gate awakening threshold; and
- whether one or two monsters are drawn when a new gate opens.

Use the actual investigator count for monster-surge size, card effects, gate
trophies required for a close-all-gates victory, and successes per doom in
final battle. This limited scope is a FAQ correction to broader wording in the
expansion books. (FAQ, PDF pp. 29, 36)

If Dunwich and Innsmouth are both in use, increase the open-gate awakening
number by 1 after applying the modified count. (Innsmouth rules, PDF p. 6;
FAQ, PDF p. 36)

### Town behavior

- "In Arkham" effects also apply in enabled expansion towns unless the effect
  says otherwise.
- Monsters on an expansion board do not count against the Arkham monster
  limit and do not go to the Outskirts.
- Expansion-town streets are adjacent to the Sky for flying movement.
- Travel between towns requires the Train Station or a depot. Spend $1 and 1
  movement point to move to the Train Station or any depot in another town.
  This does not otherwise end normal movement.
- Lost in Time and Space may normally return to an expansion location or
  street, except a Kingsport Head location or Y'ha-nthlei.

Sources: Dunwich rules, PDF p. 5; Kingsport rules, PDF p. 7; Innsmouth rules,
PDF pp. 6-7.

## Gate bursts

Gate bursts are used by several expansions and Miskatonic cards. A red gate
location is a gate burst.

- Resolve it as a normal Mythos gate result unless the location is sealed.
- At a sealed location, remove the elder sign, open a gate, and spawn a
  monster.
- Do not add doom solely for bursting that seal.
- Bursting a seal does not itself cause a monster surge.
- At a location that already has an open gate, it does cause a normal monster
  surge.
- Every gate-burst card moves all flying monsters, even if the burst is
  prevented and regardless of dimensional symbols.
- Kate Winthrop's Science! ability completely prevents a burst at her
  location; the seal remains, but the flying-monster trigger still occurs.

Sources: Dunwich rules, PDF p. 6; FAQ, PDF pp. 5, 32, 35, 39-40, 42.

## Dunwich Horror

### Board and vortices

Dunwich monsters are outside the Arkham monster limit and never enter the
Outskirts because of that limit.

A monster entering a Dunwich vortex:

1. returns to the cup;
2. raises terror by 1; and
3. adds one Dunwich Horror token if fewer than three are on the track.

Investigators can never enter a vortex. At three tokens, put the Dunwich Horror
at Sentinel Hill. (Dunwich rules, PDF p. 7)

### Dunwich Horror

- It is a Spawn monster, not part of the cup, monster limit, Outskirts, or
  trophy supply.
- Each battle draws one Dunwich Horror card defining that battle's attributes;
  printed toughness remains 5.
- On defeat, empty the track and set the marker aside. It can return if the
  track fills again.
- The defeating investigator searches one Common Item, Unique Item, Skill,
  Spell, or Ally deck and takes one card.

Sources: Dunwich rules, PDF pp. 7-8.

### Injury and Madness

When current Stamina reaches zero, the investigator may take an Injury instead
of the normal item/Clue losses:

- restore Stamina to its maximum;
- still move to St. Mary's or Lost in Time and Space as appropriate; and
- draw one Injury.

At zero Sanity, the equivalent choice draws Madness, restores Sanity to its
maximum, and still moves to Arkham Asylum or Lost in Time and Space.

Injury and Madness are not items and cannot be traded or voluntarily discarded
as items. Maximum Sanity or Stamina reaching zero still devours. An
investigator with at least two total Injury/Madness cards may retire by skipping
their turn and replacing the investigator as if devoured, without triggering
"devoured" effects. Two copies of the same Injury or Madness devour; Miskatonic
adds paired cross-type devouring conditions. (Dunwich rules, PDF p. 6;
Miskatonic rules, PDF p. 3)

### Tasks and Missions

- Steps are completed in printed order.
- End movement in the listed area and mark each completed step with a Clue
  from the supply.
- A Task step requires spending the Arkham Encounters phase at the area.
- A Mission step additionally requires its sacrifice at the start of Upkeep
  while the investigator remains there.
- Giving away or discarding the card clears its progress.
- On completion, resolve the payoff/effect, return the card to the box, and
  discard its progress tokens.

Source: Dunwich rules, PDF p. 5.

### Stalker movement

A purple-border stalker:

- moves as a normal black-border monster if no investigator is adjacent;
- otherwise ignores arrows and moves to an investigator in an adjacent street
  or unstable location;
- chooses lowest Sneak, with first player breaking ties;
- cannot enter a stable location to pursue someone.

Source: Dunwich rules, PDF pp. 6-7.

### Other Dunwich types

- Spawn monsters never enter the cup, count against the monster limit, go to
  Outskirts, or become trophies.
- An exhausted Ally still grants passive bonuses but cannot use an
  exhaust-required ability until refreshed.
- A handless weapon marked with X does not use either normal hand.
- A Condition exhausts sideways so it does not become inactive; its faceup or
  facedown state separately records active/inactive.

Sources: Dunwich rules, PDF pp. 6-7.

## Kingsport Horror

### Kingsport Head

The Causeway, Wireless Station, and Strange High House in the Mist form
Kingsport Head.

- Entering the Causeway or Wireless Station immediately ends movement.
- Unusual movement cannot move directly into a Head location.
- Lost in Time and Space cannot return there.
- Enter through Harborside and the Causeway normally.

Source: Kingsport rules, PDF p. 7.

### Aquatic movement

An orange-border aquatic monster:

- outside an aquatic location, moves as a normal black-border monster;
- in an aquatic location, moves directly to another aquatic location containing
  an investigator;
- if several qualify, chooses lowest Sneak, then first player on a tie;
- if none qualifies, moves as a normal black-border monster.

River Docks and Unvisited Isle become aquatic when this system is enabled.
(Kingsport rules, PDF pp. 7-8)

### Elusive monsters

Elusive monsters do not force combat or evasion when investigators leave or
end movement with them. To deliberately fight one during Movement, first pass
its Evade check:

- pass: combat begins;
- fail: movement ends and combat does not begin.

Elusive monsters ignore investigators for their own movement. (Kingsport
rules, PDF p. 8; FAQ, PDF p. 31)

### Rift progress

Each closed rift has a four-space track divided between two Mythos movement
patterns.

1. Whenever a Mythos card is fully resolved, compare its movement pattern to
   each closed rift.
2. For each match with an unfilled pair, draw and place one random rift
   progress marker in that pair.
3. Do nothing for a matching pair already full.
4. If all four spaces become full, open that rift at the Mythos card's gate
   location, even if it is sealed.
5. If the triggering card has no gate location, draw cards until a gate
   location is found, use only that location, and discard those extra cards.

Sources: Kingsport rules, PDF p. 8; FAQ, PDF p. 32.

### Open rifts

An open rift starts activating on the Mythos phase after it opens.

- Its marker has a dimensional symbol and a black or white color.
- When that symbol is activated, move the rift like a normal black-border
  monster using the Mythos box's arrow color, then spawn one monster at its
  resulting location.
- The symbol trigger spawns the monster even if the rift could not move.
- If the rift's printed color matches the background color that activated its
  symbol, add one doom, even if it could not move.
- A black/white board arrow does not by itself trigger doom; compare the rift
  color with the Mythos movement box.
- Rift-spawned monsters obey the usual monster limit.
- A rift entering a Dunwich or Innsmouth vortex remains there. Its later
  monster spawn occurs in the vortex and immediately resolves that vortex.

Sources: Kingsport rules, PDF pp. 8-9; FAQ, PDF pp. 31-32.

### Investigating and closing rifts

Having a true location encounter at the Kingsport location pictured on a
progress marker investigates one marker, at most one per encounter.

- For a closed rift, return that progress marker to the random supply.
- For an open rift, turn that progress marker facedown.
- When all four markers of an open rift are facedown, close the rift, return its
  rift marker to the track, and return all four progress markers to the supply.
- A location special ability is not an encounter and cannot investigate.
- A closed location cannot be investigated.

Sources: Kingsport rules, PDF p. 10; FAQ, PDF p. 32.

### Kingsport variants

- Herald/Guardian: choose at most one of each by default; their sheet text is
  active in addition to the Ancient One.
- Epic Battle: replaces the ordinary final-battle attack sequence with the
  Epic Battle deck. Each round still begins with Investigator Refresh and the
  first-player pass before drawing the next Epic Battle card.

Sources: Kingsport rules, PDF pp. 10-11; FAQ, PDF p. 33.

## Innsmouth Horror

### Restricted areas and arrest

- Devil Reef and Y'ha-nthlei are reachable only through aquatic movement and
  effects such as Falcon Point.
- An arrest in Innsmouth sends the investigator to Innsmouth Jail, loses half
  of Money rounded down, and delays even an investigator normally immune to
  delay.
- Sawbone Alley is a street area with a rescue rule, not a location.

Source: Innsmouth rules, PDF p. 6.

### Martial law

When at least half of the Ancient One's doom track is full, martial law begins
for the rest of the game. It does not end if doom later drops.

Each time an investigator ends Movement at an Innsmouth location or street with
an awareness modifier:

1. make the listed Evade check;
2. on failure, be arrested and sent to Innsmouth Jail;
3. then handle monsters at the end of movement.

An open gate at the location overrides the martial-law check for an
investigator returning through that gate. (Innsmouth rules, PDF pp. 6-7; FAQ,
PDF p. 39)

### Deep Ones Rising and vortices

The Ancient One awakens immediately if all six Deep Ones Rising spaces fill.

Add one uprising token whenever:

- a gate is prevented from opening, including by a seal or investigator
  ability; or
- a monster enters an Innsmouth vortex.

A monster entering an Innsmouth vortex returns to the cup, raises terror by 1,
and adds the uprising token. Investigators can never enter vortices.
(Innsmouth rules, PDF p. 8)

### Federal raid

During Upkeep, investigators in an Innsmouth neighborhood may spend any number
of Clues into empty Feds Raid spaces matching that neighborhood's color. When
all six spaces are filled, clear both the Feds Raid track and the Deep Ones
Rising track. Either track may begin filling again. (Innsmouth rules, PDF p. 8)

### Other Innsmouth systems

- Innsmouth Look: when instructed, shuffle the Look deck and draw the required
  number simultaneously. If the Look card is among them, resolve it, then
  return all drawn cards.
- Weapon Immunity reduces Physical and Magical Weapon combat bonuses to zero,
  but does not affect Spells.
- Spawn monsters follow the shared Spawn restrictions.
- Personal Stories have a first card with pass/fail triggers and a second
  persistent result. If pass and fail occur simultaneously, the player chooses.

Sources: Innsmouth rules, PDF pp. 7-8, 10; FAQ, PDF p. 37.

## Curse of the Dark Pharaoh, Revised Edition

Do not combine revised and original-edition components. The repository source
is the revised edition. (Dark Pharaoh rules, PDF p. 1)

### Ancient Whispers and Exhibit Encounters

- Start Ancient Whispers in Miskatonic University street.
- During Arkham Encounters, an investigator sharing its street must take an
  Exhibit Encounter.
- The encounter moves Ancient Whispers. An investigator in the destination
  street does not take another Exhibit Encounter that turn.
- At most one investigator takes an Exhibit Encounter per turn.
- If a gate occupies that street, the investigator enters the gate instead.
- If Ancient Whispers did not move during Arkham Encounters, move it in Mythos
  as a moon-symbol normal monster, ignoring investigators in its current area.
- If it already moved during Arkham Encounters, do not move it in Mythos.
- It cannot be removed and does not interact with other markers.

Source: Dark Pharaoh rules, PDF p. 2.

### Benefits, Detriments, and patrols

- Each named Benefit/Detriment is unique. Ignore an instruction to gain one
  already held by another investigator.
- One investigator may hold multiple different Benefits and Detriments.
- Return discarded cards to the available supply.
- After resolving text that applies when a Benefit or Detriment is received,
  immediately discard it if its discard condition is already met.
- A street can have at most one Patrol marker.
- Make Sneak (+0) when leaving a patrolled street and when ending Movement
  there. Failure causes arrest.
- Whenever terror rises, remove every Patrol marker.

Sources: Dark Pharaoh rules, PDF p. 2; FAQ, PDF p. 22.

The optional Dark Pharaoh Herald is a separate flag. It applies these rulebook
effects:

- gaining a Unique Item, including starting equipment, loses 1 Sanity;
- gaining an Exhibit Item requires a die roll and curses on failure;
- at start of Upkeep, each Cursed investigator loses 1 Stamina before the roll
  to remove the Curse;
- Mask monsters gain 1 toughness and add 1 doom when defeated; and
- if Nyarlathotep awakens, each Mask on the board makes his combat modifier 1
  more negative and adds one doom to his battle track.

Do not enable these effects merely because the expansion deck is in use. (Dark
Pharaoh rules, PDF p. 2)

## The King in Yellow

### Act deck

Order the Act deck from Act I through Act III. Each resolved Mythos card named
"The Next Act Begins!" attempts to put the top Act into play. Act I and Act II
have printed prevention conditions. Act III cannot be stopped once begun and
causes immediate loss. Miskatonic replaces this with a four-card sequence:
Overture, Act I, Act II, and Act III. Use the Miskatonic setup when that
overlay is enabled. (King in Yellow rules, PDF p. 1; Miskatonic rules, PDF
pp. 2-3)

Miskatonic FAQ timing: an Act trigger applies to qualifying cards drawn during
the Mythos phase even if their Mythos ability is ignored, the card is discarded
and replaced, or it was drawn for ancillary information. Give investigators the
printed opportunity to return an Act immediately after each such draw. (FAQ,
PDF p. 42)

### Magical Effects

A spell may create a named Magical Effect:

- it is not an item;
- it normally ends when its creating spell refreshes or is lost;
- it uses hands in combat as printed; and
- Magical Resistance/Immunity affects its combat bonus.

Source: King in Yellow rules, PDF p. 1.

### Setup styles and Herald

- Touring Performance stacks the expansion cards above base decks and does not
  shuffle Location decks before each draw; cycle the deck and shuffle only
  after all cards have been used.
- Permanent Performance mixes the expansion into the base decks and retains
  ordinary deck behavior.
- The King in Yellow Herald is separate. Whenever terror rises, choose a Yellow
  Sign on the doom track or the terror space just vacated. A Sign placed on
  terror brings a persistent Blight into play.
- A Blight suppresses a location encounter only when that encounter refers to
  the blighted person by name and actually means that person.
- When the Ancient One awakens, the King in Yellow Herald has no further
  effect.

Sources: King in Yellow rules, PDF p. 2; FAQ, PDF p. 27.

## The Black Goat of the Woods

### Cult encounters

An investigator with a "One of the Thousand" Cult Membership must draw from
the Cult Encounter deck instead of the normal location deck when encountering
the Black Cave, Unvisited Isle, or Woods. (Black Goat rules, PDF p. 2)

### Corruption

- Build the deck with shuffled green cards above shuffled red cards.
- Draw from the top; never reshuffle discarded Corruptions.
- Passive text is continuously active.
- Triggered text is mandatory only when both dimensional symbol and black/white
  background match the current Mythos movement box.
- Resolve Corruption triggers immediately after monster movement in Mythos
  step 3.
- Closing a gate discards every in-play Corruption matching that gate's
  dimensional symbol.
- If a Corruption must be drawn from an empty deck, awaken immediately.

Sources: Black Goat rules, PDF pp. 1-2; FAQ, PDF p. 34.

### Black Goat Herald

The optional Herald creates a normal cup and a hexagon cup. Its effects include
extra hexagon monsters at gate openings, half of surge monsters from the
hexagon cup rounded down, Corruption rewards for defeating hexagon monsters,
and doom on each surge. During a surge, alternate draws starting with the
normal cup. If a required cup is empty, draw that monster from the other cup.
With five or more investigators, an ordinary new gate receives two normal
monsters and one hexagon monster. Draws for reasons other than a new gate or
surge use the normal cup. Keep all of these effects behind the Herald flag.
(Black Goat rules, PDF p. 2; FAQ, PDF p. 35)

## The Lurker at the Threshold

### Relationships

- With two players, draw one Relationship between them.
- With three or more, each player draws one with the investigator to their
  left as partner, and benefits from their own card plus the card of the player
  to their right.
- If either partner is devoured or retires, remove both affected Relationship
  cards.
- Replacement investigators do not draw replacement Relationships.

Sources: Lurker rules, PDF pp. 1-2; FAQ, PDF p. 40.

### Replacement gate markers

Lurker gates replace all ordinary gate markers when this module is enabled.

- Devouring: an investigator at its location when it opens is devoured.
- Doom: an investigator at its location when it opens causes one extra doom.
- Endless: closing or sealing reshuffles it into the gate stack instead of
  awarding a trophy.
- Monstrous: a failed close check makes a monster appear, subject to the limit.
- Blood: a failed close check loses 1 Stamina.
- Madness: a failed close check loses 1 Sanity.
- Moving: when its dimensional symbol is activated, move as a normal monster.
  It ignores investigators as blockers, does not enter an area containing
  another gate or a vortex, pulls through and delays an investigator it moves
  onto, and removes an explored marker when it moves away. It may move during
  the same Mythos phase in which it opens.
- Split: choose either shown Other World on entry; either world may return to
  this gate; exploring one is enough to close it; closure clears monsters of
  either shown dimensional symbol.

Gates in stable locations or streets may close but never seal. (Lurker rules,
PDF p. 2; FAQ, PDF p. 40)

### Lurker Herald

Dark Pacts, Reckonings, and Power exist only with the Herald. Do not remove
Pacts or Power for insanity/unconsciousness; devouring removes both. Once the
Ancient One awakens, Power may still be spent as allowed but can no longer be
gained. (Lurker rules, PDF p. 2; FAQ, PDF pp. 40-41)

## Miskatonic Horror

Miskatonic is dependency-aware overlay content.

- Include a card only when every expansion icon it requires is enabled.
- As an alternate setup, an ineligible drawn card is returned to the box and
  replaced by another of the same type.
- Miskatonic Act cards replace, rather than supplement, King in Yellow Acts.
- Its Dunwich Horror, Blight, Look, Relationship, Reckoning, Guardian, and Epic
  Battle additions require the named parent module.

Sources: Miskatonic rules, PDF pp. 2-3.

### Alternate gate and Clue locations

- For a two-location gate, use the upper location when its board is in play;
  otherwise use the lower location.
- A gate burst applies only to the selected location.
- For alternate Clue locations, use the first if its board is active, otherwise
  the second.
- Ignore a single Clue placement whose expansion board is not active.

Source: Miskatonic rules, PDF p. 4.

### Injury/Madness pairs and variants

- Each new Injury has a corresponding Madness, and vice versa. Holding a
  corresponding pair devours the investigator unless a specific ability
  prevents it.
- Battle Event Epic cards resolve and immediately draw another card.
- Battle Condition Epic cards remain active, then immediately draw another.
- Institutions are optional sheets, normally at most one, whose printed rules
  apply alongside at most one Herald and one Guardian.
- The Dunwich Horror Herald requires the Dunwich board.

Sources: Miskatonic rules, PDF pp. 3-4.
